Singer’s Bio

Born Robert Calvin Bland in January 27, 1930, in Rosemark, Tennessee, Bobby Bland was a legendary American blues singer, widely regarded as one of the most influential blues icons of the 20th century. Bland’s family moved to Memphis when he was only seven, where he grew up listening to blues and gospel music. Bland began singing gospel music at his local church, and by the age of 19, he joined the Beale Streeters, a group that included legendary musicians such as B.B. King and Junior Parker.

Age and Relationships

Bland passed away on June 23, 2013, at the age of 83. Bland was married twice in his life, first to Josephine Wallace in 1952, who passed away in 1991, and then to Willie Martin in 1992, who passed away in 2011. Though Bland remained mostly private about his relationships, he’s known for having several high-profile relationships with women throughout his lifetime.

Children and Height

Bland had six children with different partners, and several grandchildren. His eldest son committed suicide in 1972, but he remained close to his other children throughout his lifetime. He was also known for his impressive height of 6’1.

Career

Bland’s music career spanned over six decades, during which he released more than 30 studio albums. Bland is famously known for his unique blend of blues, R&B, and soul music, which earned him the title ‘The King of the Blues.’ Bland’s distinctive voice and emotional depth made him a hit with audiences, and he quickly gained recognition as one of the most talented and versatile singers of his time.

Top Songs

Throughout his career, Bland recorded several iconic songs that became classics of blues and soul music. Among his top songs are ‘It’s My Life Baby’, ‘Turn On Your Love Light’, ‘That’s The Way Love Is’, ‘Stormy Monday Blues’, and ‘Farther Up the Road.’ His signature song, ‘Ain’t No Love In The Heart Of The City,’ was later remade by other singers, including Whitesnake and Jay-Z.

Net Worth

Bland’s net worth was estimated to be around $2 million at the time of his death. However, his influence and impact on the music industry are immeasurable, and his legacy continues to inspire artists and audiences alike.

Achievements and Awards

Over the years, Bland received numerous accolades and awards for his contribution to the music industry. He was inducted into the Blues Hall of Fame in 1981, and the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1992. Bland was also honored with the NEA National Heritage Fellowship and a Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award in 1997.

Legacy

Bobby Bland’s influence on modern music cannot be overstated. As one of the most iconic blues singers of the 20th century, Bland inspired countless musicians and singers, including Mick Jagger and Van Morrison. Bland’s music continues to be celebrated and listened to by fans around the world, and his legacy lives on through his recordings and the artists he has influenced.

In the words of music journalist Peter Guralnick, “Bobby Bland brought the blues out of the Delta and into the mainstream. He was a giant, a king among men, and a true artist whose artistry transcended his time and place. He will be missed, but his music will live on.”
